Our Journey

A History of Growth & Innovation

From a small recycling operation to Israel's most recognised environmental industry group.

1974

Alon Metal — The Beginning

Founded in the early 1970s, the company became a leading manufacturer of waste containers, vehicles and a wide range of products — marking a new chapter in Israeli environmental management.

1994

Y.R.A.V — Environmental Industries

Y.R.A.V becomes the largest waste collection company in Israel, listed on the Israeli Stock Exchange with 12 transfer stations, 1,000 workers and 300 trucks.

2000

Acquired by Veolia

Y.R.A.V is sold to Veolia (VIOLA), reflecting the company's scale and strategic value in the Israeli waste management market.

2000

KMM — Recycling Plants

Collection and preparation for recycling of many raw materials — including paper, cardboard, plastic and more. Operates 4 processing plants across the country.

2014

Acquisition of Tal-El

KMM acquires "Tal-El," expanding its processing capacity and adding key infrastructure in the Nitzanei Oz industrial park.

2015

Tire Recycling

The T.M.Z. plant is established in Beersheba, processing and shredding end-of-life tires for a wide range of secondary uses.

2022

Establishment of Tamir Plant

An agreement is signed to establish a new packaging sorting plant with Tamir Recycling Corporation. KMM is sold to Generation Capital, serving 550 workers, 150 trucks, and 90 municipalities.

2024

Cycle — Circular Economy

Collection operations begin for Cycle, Israel's largest deposit bottle collection company — collecting from thousands of points nationwide, placing return machines in supermarkets and parks across the country, and enabling implementation of the deposit law.
